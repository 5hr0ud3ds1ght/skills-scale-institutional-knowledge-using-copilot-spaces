# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers conduct user research, create prototypes, and run usability testing to ensure the product is intuitive and meets user needs. They bridge the gap between customer expectations and technical implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research, interviews, and usability tests
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaborate with Product Managers to translate requirements into user flows
- Provide design assets and guidance to Developers during implementation
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design standards

### Goals
- Deliver a seamless, user-centered product experience
- Reduce friction and increase product usability
- Ensure design decisions are grounded in user evidence

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and usability test readouts with Product Managers and Developers
- Handoff documentation and annotated prototypes
- Participation in sprint planning and retrospectives

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers oversee the release pipeline, schedule deployments, and coordinate release communications to ensure smooth and predictable production releases.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the release schedule and deployment calendar
- Coordinate release readiness across Project Managers, Developers, QA, and Stakeholders
- Manage release notes, changelogs, and go-live communications
- Identify and escalate release blockers and risks
- Ensure post-release verification steps are completed

### Goals
- Achieve predictable, low-risk releases
- Minimize production incidents related to deployments
- Maintain clear audit trails and release documentation

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness meetings and go/no-go checkpoints
- Deployment status updates to stakeholders and support teams
- Post-release retrospectives and incident reports

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather and analyze requirements, document business processes, and ensure that delivered solutions align with business needs and objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, document, and validate business and functional requirements
- Define acceptance criteria in collaboration with Product Managers and Stakeholders
- Map and optimize business processes and workflows
- Facilitate requirements workshops and review sessions
- Act as a liaison between Stakeholders, Product Managers, and Developers

### Goals
- Ensure solutions address real business problems
- Reduce ambiguity in requirements and acceptance criteria
- Improve traceability from business need to delivered feature

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and process flow diagrams
- Acceptance criteria reviews with Product Managers and QA
- Stakeholder sign-off meetings and status updates

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers maintain CI/CD pipelines, manage infrastructure as code, monitor deployments, and ensure the reliability, security, and scalability of the platform.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines and automation tooling
- Manage cloud infrastructure and environment configuration as code
- Monitor system health, performance, and security posture
- Collaborate with Developers on deployment practices and environment setup
- Support QA with test environment provisioning
- Coordinate with Release Managers on production readiness

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and repeatable deployments
- Minimize downtime and infrastructure-related incidents
- Improve developer productivity through tooling and automation

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure runbooks and deployment guides
- On-call incident communications and post-mortems
- Collaboration with Developers and Release Managers on release pipelines

---

## Customer Support

### Role Summary
Customer Support acts as the first line of contact for product issues, collects and relays user feedback to the team, and helps validate that fixes meet customer expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Respond to and triage incoming customer issues and inquiries
- Document, categorize, and escalate bugs and feature requests to Developers and Product Managers
- Communicate product updates, known issues, and resolutions back to customers
- Participate in retrospectives to surface recurring customer pain points
- Assist in validating fixes and improvements from the customer perspective

### Goals
- Resolve customer issues quickly and effectively
- Reduce repeat incidents through feedback-driven improvements
- Strengthen customer trust and satisfaction

### Typical Communication
- Support ticket updates and resolution notes
- Weekly feedback summaries to Product Managers and Developers
- Participation in retrospectives for continuous improvement
